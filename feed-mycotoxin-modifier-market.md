<p><p>The Feed Mycotoxin Modifier market is divided into two main types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic mycotoxin modifiers typically include mineral-based agents, such as clay and silica, which absorb and bind mycotoxins, rendering them ineffective. Organic modifiers, on the other hand, consist of natural compounds like plant extracts, probiotics, and enzymes that not only detoxify mycotoxins but may also enhance animal health and feed efficiency. Both types aim to improve animal performance and safety in livestock production.</p></p>
