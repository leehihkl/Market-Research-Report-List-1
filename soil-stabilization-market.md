<p><p>Soil stabilization is essential for enhancing soil properties in construction projects. The market is segmented into three main types: mechanical, chemical, and thermal. Mechanical stabilization involves physical techniques such as compaction and mixing to improve soil strength. Chemical stabilization uses additives like lime or cement to alter soil characteristics chemically. Thermal stabilization employs heat to modify soil properties, often in expansive clay soils. Each method caters to different soil challenges, improving durability and structural integrity for various applications.</p></p>
