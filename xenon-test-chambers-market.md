<p><p>Xenon test chambers simulate natural sunlight exposure to evaluate the durability of materials and products. The market for these chambers is primarily divided into two types: air cooling and water cooling. Air cooling systems use fans to circulate air, maintaining optimal temperatures, while water cooling systems utilize water to dissipate heat more efficiently, offering better temperature stability. Each type caters to different testing requirements, with water cooling often preferred for extended test durations and larger samples due to its superior thermal management.</p></p>
